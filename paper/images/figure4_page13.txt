## Pertinent Paper Text
Section IV (Training Details) / Section V (Ablation): "We study the effect of training horizon T on test performance. Training with longer horizons (T=8) consistently improves CLS-NR across all test horizons. The diagonal (train=test) improves monotonically with horizon length. Training T=1 achieves only 75.79 on test T=1, while T=8 achieves 94.07 on test T=8."

"The paper uses T=8 as the default training horizon based on this analysis."

## Architectural Details
- Training horizon T: number of auto-regressive steps during training (1, 3, 5, or 8)
- Testing horizon: number of steps during closed-loop evaluation
- CLS-NR: Closed-Loop Score Non-Reactive (primary metric)
- Best performance: train T=8, test T=8 → CLS-NR=94.07
- Short training horizon (T=1) hurts long-horizon test performance most
- Default paper setting: T=8 for both train and test
