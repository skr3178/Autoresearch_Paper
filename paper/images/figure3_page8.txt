## Pertinent Paper Text
Section V (Experiments): "Qualitative results show CarPlanner (RL) achieves consistently high per-step scores across diverse scenarios, while PDM-Closed and CarPlanner-IL fail in challenging cases (S-TTC=0 for IL indicates time-to-collision violations)."

"CLS-NR (Closed-Loop Score, Non-Reactive) is the primary metric. CarPlanner achieves 95.14 vs PDM-Closed's 47.40 — a ~2× improvement."

## Metric Definitions (from figure)
- CLS-NR: Closed-Loop Score Non-Reactive (primary metric, higher is better)
- S-CR: Collision Rate score
- S-TTC: Time-To-Collision score
- S-Area: Drivable area score
- S-PR: Progress score

## Key Takeaway
RL training (CarPlanner) dramatically improves CLS-NR (47.40 → 95.14) and S-PR (31.08 → 85.07) over both PDM-Closed and the IL-only variant. S-TTC=0 for IL indicates the IL model causes collision-risk scenarios that RL fixes.
